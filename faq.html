Frequently asked questions

Everything you need to know about Chariot. Can't find your answer? Contact support →

Getting started

How does Chariot track my commute automatically? +
Chariot uses iOS geofencing — invisible boundaries around your home, work, and transit hubs. When your iPhone crosses one of these boundaries, iOS wakes Chariot up and logs the event. No tapping, no starting or stopping. Once you've set up your locations, Chariot works completely in the background.
What do I need to set up before Chariot starts tracking? +
The setup wizard walks you through everything: (1) Add your Home and Work locations, (2) Add any transit hubs like train stations or bus depots, (3) Add the transport modes you use (car, train, bus, walking), and (4) Build your first route connecting these waypoints. Once done, Chariot tracks automatically.
Why does Chariot need "Always" location permission? +
iOS only delivers geofence events to apps that have "Always" location access. With "While Using" permission, Chariot can only see your location when the app is open — it can't detect departures or arrivals when your phone is in your pocket. "Always" access is required for automatic background tracking. Chariot does not track your location continuously — it only receives events when you cross a geofence boundary.
What is a geofence? +
A geofence is an invisible circular boundary around a location. When your iPhone enters or exits that boundary, iOS fires an event. Chariot uses these events to know when you've left home, arrived at a transit hub, or reached your workplace. You can set the radius for each geofence — larger radii for busy areas where GPS can be less precise, smaller for tightly defined locations.
Does Chariot work if I don't commute every day? +
Yes. You set Active Days for each waypoint — for example, Monday through Thursday only. Chariot will only respond to geofence events on those days, within the time windows you set. If you don't commute on a Friday, your Friday geofence events are ignored.

Tracking & geofences

Why didn't my commute get tracked today? +
A few common causes: (1) Location permission may have changed to "While Using" — check iPhone Settings → Chariot → Location. (2) The commute fell outside your arrival or departure time window. (3) Low Power Mode was active, which can delay background location events. (4) The app was force-quit — iOS suspends background capabilities for force-quit apps. Try leaving Chariot running in the background rather than swiping it away.
What is "drive-through" detection? +
If you drive past a geofence without stopping — for example, driving by a bus station you're not using that day — Chariot detects the rapid enter-then-exit and ignores both events. It requires you to dwell inside a geofence for at least 60 seconds before treating it as a real arrival. This prevents false session restarts when you drive past a waypoint.
How do I make tracking more reliable? +
A few things help: (1) Set generous time windows — if you usually arrive at 8:30 AM, set your arrival window to 6:00–11:00 AM to give iOS room to deliver events slightly late. (2) Increase your geofence radius — 100–150m works well for most locations; larger for busy city streets where GPS is less precise. (3) Keep Background App Refresh enabled for Chariot in iPhone Settings → General → Background App Refresh. (4) Don't force-quit Chariot.
What is a "Destination" vs a "Route Marker" waypoint? +
A Destination is a place you actually stop at — home, work, or a transit hub where you board. Chariot requires a minimum dwell time before counting your arrival. A Route Marker is a point along your path that confirms which route you took — you pass through it without stopping. Driving through a Route Marker fires its event immediately, without the dwell requirement.

Cost calculations

How does Chariot calculate my commute cost? +
Chariot calculates cost per leg of your commute using the transport mode assigned to that leg. For driving legs: fuel cost = miles driven × (price per gallon ÷ MPG), plus parking and tolls. For transit legs: ticket cost based on your payment type (per-ride, monthly pass, ride pack, etc.) divided into a per-trip rate. For walk/bike legs: no cash cost, just time value. All legs also include time value = duration × your hourly rate.
What is "True Cost"? +
True Cost = Cash Cost + Time Value. Cash Cost is what you actually spend — fares, fuel, parking, tolls. Time Value is what your commuting time is worth in dollars, based on your hourly rate in Settings. Most people significantly underestimate their commute cost when they only count cash. If your commute costs $15 in fares but takes 90 minutes and your time is worth $60/hour, your True Cost is $15 + $90 = $105 — for a single day.
Why is my cost showing $0.00? +
Cost calculation requires two things to be set up: (1) Your cost settings in the Settings tab — fuel efficiency, gas price, transit ticket type, time value per hour, and parking rate. (2) Route legs must have been completed with an associated transport mode. If either is missing, costs will show $0. Go to Settings and verify all relevant cost fields are filled in.
When I change my cost settings, does it update past commutes? +
No — cost changes apply to future commutes only. Historical commutes retain the costs that were calculated when they completed. This ensures your history is accurate and reflects what you actually spent at the time.
How does Chariot handle monthly or annual transit passes? +
Chariot divides your pass cost into an estimated per-trip rate. Monthly pass: cost ÷ 40 estimated rides. Annual pass: cost ÷ 480 estimated rides. Ride pack: pack cost ÷ number of rides in the pack. This gives you a consistent per-commute cost to compare against your driving alternative.

Routes & legs

What's the difference between a waypoint, a hub, a leg, and a route? +
A waypoint is any location with a geofence — home, work, a train station, a bus stop. A hub is any waypoint that isn't home or work (a transit stop, pickup spot, etc.). A leg is one segment of your journey from one waypoint to the next, with a specific transport mode. A route is a named collection of legs that describes a complete one-way commute — for example, "Home to Citi HQ via Downtown."
Can I have different routes for different days? +
Yes. You can create as many routes as you need — one for when you drive, one for when you take the train, one for when you work from a different office. Chariot automatically detects which route best matches the waypoints you hit on any given day.
How many routes and waypoints can I create? +
There's no hard limit set by Chariot. However, iOS limits any single app to 20 geofences at a time. Chariot manages this automatically — if you have more than 20 waypoints, it prioritizes the ones most likely to be active based on your schedule and time windows.

Text alerts & email summaries

How do text alerts work? +
Text alerts let you notify a contact (like a family member) when you arrive at or depart from a waypoint. Because iOS prevents apps from sending SMS silently in the background, Chariot uses iOS Shortcuts as the delivery mechanism. You set up a one-time Shortcuts automation that calls Chariot's message template and sends the text. After that, it works automatically with no interaction required.
Why do I need to set up iOS Shortcuts for text alerts? +
Apple's iOS platform does not allow third-party apps to send SMS messages silently in the background — this is a platform privacy restriction, not a Chariot limitation. iOS Shortcuts has broader system permissions that allow it to send messages automatically. Chariot exposes your message templates and recipient information to Shortcuts via App Intents, and Shortcuts handles the actual delivery. This keeps your data on-device while still enabling background alerts.
What variables can I use in my alert messages? +
Chariot supports these variables in your message templates: %time% (current time), %location% (waypoint name), %address% (waypoint address), %date% (today's date), %datetime% (date and time), %route% (detected route name), %duration% (commute duration so far). For example: "Arriving at %location% at %time%, taking %route%."
How do daily and weekly email summaries work? +
Like text alerts, email summaries use iOS Shortcuts for delivery — keeping everything on-device with no server involved. You set up a time-based Shortcuts automation that calls Chariot's summary App Intent and sends the email. The daily summary covers that day's commutes, costs, and notes. The weekly summary covers the full week with day-by-day breakdown, route frequency, and week-over-week comparison.

Privacy & data

Does Chariot share my data with anyone? +
No. Chariot has no server. Your commute data, location history, cost information, and notes never leave your iPhone. We have no ability to access your data because it was never sent to us in the first place.
Where is my data stored? +
All data is stored locally on your iPhone using Apple's SwiftData framework. After each completed commute, Chariot also writes a CSV backup to your iCloud Drive (in a Chariot folder), which syncs to your other Apple devices through your personal iCloud account. We never see this data — iCloud is your personal cloud storage.
Can I export my data? +
Yes. Chariot automatically saves a CSV file to your iCloud Drive after every completed commute. You can also manually export from Settings → Sync & Backup → Export to CSV, which gives you an iOS share sheet to send the file anywhere — email, AirDrop, Dropbox, or any other app.
How do I delete all my data? +
Go to Settings → Your Data → Delete All My Data. This permanently removes all sessions, waypoints, routes, transport modes, and preferences from your device. This action cannot be undone. The CSV files already saved to your iCloud Drive are not affected — you can delete those manually from the Files app.
Does Chariot use a server or cloud backend? +
No. Chariot is a fully local app. There is no Chariot server, no Chariot cloud, and no Chariot account. Your data lives on your device and in your personal iCloud Drive (which you control). If you delete the app, your data is gone — no server copy exists.

Pricing & trial

How long is the free trial? +
30 days. Full access to all features, no credit card required. After 30 days, you'll need to choose a plan to continue tracking. Your historical data is preserved — nothing is deleted when the trial ends.
What happens when my trial ends? +
Chariot shows you a paywall with your own trial stats — commutes tracked, money spent, time lost — so you can see exactly what you'd be giving up. You can then choose Annual ($9.99/year), Lifetime ($19.99 one-time), or cancel. Your existing data is never deleted.
Can I restore my purchase on a new device? +
Yes. Purchases are tied to your Apple ID. On a new device, download Chariot and tap "Restore Purchase" on the paywall screen. Your purchase will be restored at no charge. Note: your commute history is local to each device — it does not transfer automatically, though your CSV exports in iCloud Drive remain accessible.

Troubleshooting

My commute shows "In Progress" but I'm already home. +
This means the arrival geofence at your destination didn't fire. Most common causes: (1) The geofence radius is too small — try increasing it to 150–200m. (2) You arrived outside your arrival time window — widen the window in Edit Location. (3) iOS didn't deliver the event due to Low Power Mode or the app being suspended. You can manually mark a commute complete using the "Change Time" button on the Commute page.
My stats show $0 even though I've set up my costs. +
Cost calculation requires completed legs with associated transport modes. If your session completed but no legs were tracked (because no intermediate geofences fired), the cost will be $0. Check that your route legs are set up correctly and that your hub geofences are active. Also verify that cost settings are entered in Settings — fuel price, MPG, transit ticket cost, and time value per hour.
I don't see a Chariot folder in iCloud Drive. +
The Chariot folder and CSV file are only created after your first completed commute session. If no commute has fully completed yet (meaning both departure and arrival geofences fired), the file won't exist. Also check that iCloud Drive is enabled on your iPhone in Settings → [your name] → iCloud → iCloud Drive.
The app isn't tracking my commute in the background. +
Check all of these: (1) iPhone Settings → Chariot → Location → set to "Always". (2) iPhone Settings → Chariot → Location → Precise Location → ON. (3) iPhone Settings → General → Background App Refresh → Chariot → ON. (4) Low Power Mode is OFF. (5) Chariot is not force-quit — leave it running in the background. If all of these are correct and tracking still fails, please contact support.
